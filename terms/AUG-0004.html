Zero-Point Self [ZPS]

Term code: AUG-0004 · Discipline: General Phenomenology (KG) · Period: 1 · Language: de

Description

Ausgangszustand des Wissens vor jeglicher KI-Nutzung. Persoenliche Referenzlinie.

Operational definition

Zero-Point Self is recorded when a participant reports a baseline identity-state distinct from AI-augmented states, articulated as the self-experience present in the absence of any external cognitive aid. Operationalised via a structured self-report protocol contrasting augmented and unaugmented sessions.

Measurement schema

Within-subject A/B design: two 30-minute reflection sessions (A=AI-augmented, B=unaided), each followed by a 12-item identity-distinctness questionnaire (5-point Likert). Primary metric: B-A difference in self-continuity sub-scale.

Inclusion criteria

Both sessions completed with valid questionnaire responses and a discernible state-contrast.

Exclusion criteria

Sessions where the unaided condition was contaminated by incidental AI use (logged via device-monitoring).

The Companion Shift [CPS]

Term code: AUG-0539 · Discipline: RL (RL) · Period: 2 · Language: de

Description

Der beobachtbare Wandel in der Wahrnehmung der KI: vom statischen Werkzeug zum flexiblen Arbeitsinstrument.

Operational definition

A Companion Shift is recorded when a participant's framing of an AI system transitions from task-tool to ongoing companion across a longitudinal window, evidenced by changes in language (pronoun use, naming, conversational openings) and by self-reported relational orientation on a validated scale.

Measurement schema

Longitudinal linguistic-feature tracking (pronoun frequency, address-form use, opening-greeting types) across at least 8 weeks of session-logs, paired with monthly Inclusion-of-Other-in-Self (IOS) scale (1-7). Shift detected when IOS rises by at least 2 points and pronoun-personalisation more than doubles.

Example data

{
  "participant_id": "P033",
  "weeks_observed": 10,
  "ios_week1": 2,
  "ios_week10": 5,
  "personal_pronoun_rate_w1": 0.08,
  "personal_pronoun_rate_w10": 0.22,
  "named_assistant": true
}

Inclusion criteria

IOS increase at least 2 points plus more than doubled personalisation-rate over the observation window.

Exclusion criteria

Cases where the participant explicitly role-plays a companion frame from session one.
